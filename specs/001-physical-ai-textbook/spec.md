Students will gain hands-on experience with ROS 2, Gazebo, Unity, and NVIDIA Isaac™, culminating in the ability to design, simulate, and deploy AI-driven robotic systems.\n\n## Module 1: The Robotic Nervous System (ROS 2)\n- Introduction to ROS 2 architecture and core concepts\n- ROS 2 packages, nodes, topics, services, and actions\n- Building and running ROS 2 applications\n- Robot kinematics and dynamics with ROS 2\n- Sensor integration and data processing\n\n## Module 2: The Digital Twin (Gazebo & Unity)\n- Introduction to robot simulation environments\n- Gazebo for realistic physics-based simulation\n- Unity for high-fidelity rendering and synthetic data generation\n- Bridging ROS 2 with Gazebo and Unity\n- Developing and testing robot algorithms in simulation\n\n## Module 3: The AI-Robot Brain (NVIDIA Isaac™)\n- Overview of NVIDIA Isaac Platform for robotics development\n- Isaac ROS for accelerating perception and AI tasks\n- Isaac Sim for high-fidelity simulation and synthetic data generation\n- Integrating advanced AI models with robotic systems\n- Real-world deployment considerations with Isaac\n\n## Module 4: Vision-Language-Action (VLA)\n- Principles of Vision-Language Models (VLMs) in robotics\n- Integrating large language models (LLMs) for natural language interaction\n- Action planning and execution based on VLM outputs\n- Human-robot interaction and collaboration\n- **Capstone Project**: Develop an intelligent robot agent capable of performing complex tasks using VLA principles in a simulated or physical environment.\n\n## Why Physical AI Matters\nPhysical AI is at the forefront of technological innovation, bridging the gap between artificial intelligence and the real world. It enables robots to perceive, reason, and act in complex, unstructured environments, leading to breakthroughs in automation, healthcare, exploration, and human assistance. Understanding physical AI is crucial for developing the next generation of intelligent machines that can safely and effectively interact with humans and their surroundings.\n\n## Learning Outcomes\n1.  Understand the core concepts of ROS 2 for robotic system development.\n2.  Utilize Gazebo and Unity for creating and simulating digital twins of robots.\n3.  Implement AI-driven robot behaviors using NVIDIA Isaac™ and Isaac ROS.\n4.  Develop Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models for advanced robot intelligence.\n5.  Design and execute complex robotic tasks in simulation and potentially on physical hardware.\n6.
